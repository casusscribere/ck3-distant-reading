Alas, no. Playable scheme agents were one of our stretch goals for this DLC, but they were scrapped about midway through development in favour of iteration time. Never say never and all that, but they're a tad unlikely now. A good deal of re-writing to the outcome events, though no new chains I'm afraid. We actually took specifically this out: you got a choice before but it was just selected randomly and there was never a reason to select anything other than "yes, whatever I just random rolled". The way it was set up was causing all kinds of fractal complexity down the line in how balancing saves and such went, so what we're hoping is that by taking out a redundant choice (and thus allowing us to simplify the script down markedly), it'll be much easier to add additional ways to die/avoid death in future. I'm not aware offhand about the fix, I'm afraid. I recall hearing some buzz behind me but last week was a bit of a busy one ^^'. Gotta get our final fixes in and send the build off for certification and such. Story content does indeed fire if you don't play as them, though to varying degrees of success. El Cid's got a decent chance of going through his chain successfully, Hereward is basically screwed if he's an AI (which, also historical), and Hassan'll get some of the way through before generally knackering it on the end stretch. IIRC: no, because he doesn't have them yet by 1066. I'll look into it! Per the DD, He's a bookmark character even. I think we broadened his achievement out to be doable by other chars as a result. Damnation, I know I did add several, not sure if I've got a list to hand. I do remember your post picked up something vital though — we didn't have the final Fatimid caliph!
