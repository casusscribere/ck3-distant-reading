Paradox, i´m not at all impressed with legitimacy, as i´ve already outlined in this thread: https://forum.paradoxplaza.com/foru...g-legitimacy-to-titles-and-claimants.1624141/ With all due respect, i think that it´s a very badly designed mechanic. 1.) It´s illogical on many levels. Why should Vassals expect different tiers of legitimacy for different rulers? Be it Duke or King, vassals who care about something like legitimacy would want to have the most legitimate liege as possible, "Ah, my liege murdered his way to dukehood and is an incompetent fool, but i guess he´s good enough, eh?" But more importantly- Legitimacy should be on a per title basis. Example given in the thread i started a few days ago- You can be the legitimate Ruler of kingdom A, invade Kingdom b, and might become even more legitimate in Kingdom A, for being a successful warrior, but the counts and dukes of Kingdom b would see you as somebody absolutely illegitimate. This is the very question you raise when starting to explain legitimacy- You might be a renowned king of the Capetian Dynasty, but are you the rightful emperor of byzantium? No, you are not. You´d need two different legitimacy values in order to portray this dynamic. But with the new system you either are the legitimate ruler of both or of neither. 2.) Furthermore, only rulers having legitimacy makes no sense. All claimants to a title should have a certain claim to legitimacy, which would matter for things like claimant factions or pressing claims. Sure, keeping track of this might be too much, but even limiting it to kingdoms and empires and in a limited way might be enough. 3.) In doing so it also fails to fullfill the goal you´ve formulated for itself, which is to consolidate various systems. Instead it adds another system, which doesn´t interact properly with others. We still have the basic claim system of weak and strong claims, while it should, logically, be consolidated with the legitimacy system. By not tracking those, it also keeps claimant factions illogical; Because all claimants are considered equally valid in comparison to you. I´m sure there will be other ways it will be inconsistent. 4.) It, in itself, is badly designed, because it looks like another "Bar" system. Low values bad, high values good. And while there will be some decisions to make, like, do i disinherit my child and lower my legitimacy? It still looks like a snowball system. Once you are doing well, you will have a high legitimacy and get the associated bonuses. The examples you have presented are all things which become easier when you are doing well. (Having higher tier parents, having the money/ability to hold feasts and to create legends, win wars..) While there will be some jumps in Legitimacy, for example a plague wiping out your direct heirs and a distant cousing inheriting, on average i´m certain that it will be a "Win more" system. 5.) It fails to connect with CK3s foundation, which ought to be the relationship between characters. Whether somebody was considered the legitimate ruler or not was a subjective matter, with all vassals having differing opinions. Your dynasty matters, yes, but also your religion, your upbringing, your skill as a warrior.. It was a highly relational affair, just like feudalism was. By portraying legitimacy as something disassociated from the opinions of the mighty within your realm you do this complex and interesting system, and your game, a huge disfavor. In that way the opinion system so far is a better representation of legitimacy than the new system.
