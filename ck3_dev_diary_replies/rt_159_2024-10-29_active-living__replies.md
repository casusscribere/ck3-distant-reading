The counterbalance is the opportunity cost to achieve these green positive modifiers, which requires you to forego gaining the green positive modifiers available from other Lifestyle trees. Adjustments to the challenge level of the game need to be done in a way that affects existing content, not just new content. If the Wandering tree isn't in line, power-wise, with the other Lifestyle trees, very few players will use it and the value of the DLC will be diminished in their eyes.. Also, raising the general challenge level is not something that's going to be offered in a DLC, unless its a DLC specifically aimed at hardcore fans and marketed specifically as raising the difficulty level. Casual gamers aren't going to be impressed if, in order to get new events and a new Lifestyle, they have to also accept an increased difficulty level. They're two different things that appeal to two different parts of the customer base.
