Waaaaaaay ahead of you . Heya folks! Harm events designer & axer here, and I can see we've got some people upset, so I'll give little context on why this was done: harm events weren't actually an intended part of EP2. They weren't an intended part of anything. They were something I made in my own time because there seemed to be a clear dislike from a vocal part of the community (whose size I think I probably overestimated) for ruler longevity, and I thought I had a good way to help start addressing that. Problem is, though they really worked for certain types of players, it's rapidly become clear that they're a huge net negative for many more. They're materially unpopular with most players, something reflected in a lot of the feedback we've had since Tours & Tournaments launched: I frequently see them described as misguided at best, often lazy or badly designed, sometimes as just a plaster over deeper gameplay problems or as a rush job thrown in with no real care, and worst of all, as a huge source of quit moments for new players. Though I don't really agree with the harsher criticisms, they definitely ended up a markedly larger pain point than the one they were trying to solve, and for many more people. So they're now adjusted for wider taste odds-wise and disabled for players by default. If folks still want them, they're there, they can be turned back on as easily as they could be turned off before, but they were an idea that just didn't work out how I'd hoped. Sucks but it happens. We've not abandoned the idea of making characters less long-lived or providing more setbacks and player friction, but we (or I, here, I suppose) don't think that harm events are actually such a good way to do that. We'll continue to look at other ways to address health and longevity issues, and modifier stacking generally, but likely aiming for stuff that's a bit subtler/more broadly applicable. I'm just not expecting to see the list expanded or get the time allocated to make the generic health loss harm events that I'd hoped to get to do at some point. If there proves to be sufficient appetite for it, we might add a game rule to revert the survival chances to the original values, but if I'm being frank then even within the small minority of people who liked harm events, the vast majority of those seemed to want increased odds too, so I'm afraid that folks who want harsh chances and random death (at least, in this specific way: again we'll be trying other stuff in future, just not this) are in something of a super-minority. Honestly, I'd personally agree with that assessment of game rule configuration. Hit a good deal of the nail on the head there, though I will stress that we've also had a lot of complaints from non-newbs too. I suspect that a portion of it comes down to what's realistic not necessarily being immersive and vice versa, as well as immersion varying between player types — sometimes substantially.
