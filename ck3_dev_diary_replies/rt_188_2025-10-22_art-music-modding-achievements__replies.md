## Reply 22 — participant_003 · 2025-10-22 · post-30815797

Since you mentioned council modding, i think there might never be a better time to mention the hardest thing to mod in the council. Namely the GUI. Currently, there is no datamodel for valid council positions. So you have to add them manually to GUI, by blocks and hide or show them with "visible = " parameter. Not only this requires to have a duplicate trigger that essentially has the same contents as council_position's "valid_position" parameter, but it also requires a tremendous amount of planning to pull off. Thats because council positions are in two columns. So if one is having it's "visible" unfulfilled, the second column gets resized to use that extra space. So if there is lets say composition like this: 1 2 3 4 5 6 And position 4 isn't valid for the liege, then it turns into 1 2 wide 3 5 6 Instead of optimum scenario which would be: 1 2 3 5 6 So when modders are designing new council position, they can't really make just one of them special. They have to make them in pairs. They also can't really have one council position disabled for special cases if they have no replacement in mind. The whole problem with triggers being tied to "visible" is even more messy when we take into account that you don't always look at your own council - you can look at your liege's too. So the triggers get extra funky. Making the window just access datamodel, and grab all positions that fulfill valid_position would be so much more healthy for council modding and we'd probably see much more of it.

I'm probably too late with that, but in case any of the devs sees this, there is one big issue with characterhistory. effects from effect.log, regardless if they were properly put into effect = {} bracket, or if they were just written without it, aren't executed from character's entry if that character is loaded as dead. This is a big issue, because there are lots of things that can only be executed as an effect. Biggest example - adding xp to leveled trait. If character was legendary blademaster when he was alive, in the bookmarks in which he is dead, he will be displayed as aspiring blademaster, because he will get trait - but not the xp. Our AGOT mod has hundreds of extra lines devoted to combating and workarounding this issue, but even that can't defeat it fully. Crusader Kings 2 eventually got extra history command - "effect_even_if_dead = {}" that was executed regardless of aliveliness of loaded character. It would be very welcome if that effect was added to CK3 as well. If that would be too troublesome however, at least part of the issue can be mitigated if we could iterate over all dead characters in on_game_start. The amount of iterators that iterate over dead people however, is really small. There is parameter "even_if_dead = yes" that can be added to certain scopes (like father/mother/every_child etc.) but critically can't be added to things like every_dynasty_member or every_living_character. Developing history for mods with multiple bookmarks is heavily handicapped by this issue.
