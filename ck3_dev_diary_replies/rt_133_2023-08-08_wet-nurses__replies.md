And using a wet nurse is also bad for the mother's health. Breastfeeding prevents pregnancy (this is known as lactational amenorrhea)§ and pregnancy was very dangerous in this era. This is the reason that wet nurses had a huge effect on medieval history: not because of anything they did, but because their noble mistresses got pregnant again soon after giving birth, often before their bodies had fully recovered. At best, this meant succession was complicated by noble families with many, sickly infants. At worst, the resulting maternal mortality meant marital alliances had to be reconfigured when men re-married, never mind all the stresses and complications and heartache of seeing your spouse or mother pass away and having to adjust to being or having a step-parent. So I am disappointed that this feature omits the most important personal and political dynamic of wet nurses. It's not a bad feature, but it would be more realistic and more interesting game play if using Wet Nurses increased maternal fertility. § I am sure that CK2 had a trait that reduced fertility just after birth to reflect this, but I can't find anything in the wiki so maybe it was from a mod.
