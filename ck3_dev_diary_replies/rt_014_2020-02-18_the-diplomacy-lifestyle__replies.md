> durbal said: So how does, for example, not being able to make an alliance without a marriage not also hinder 'roleplay'? Click to expand...
<!-- artifact:quote:end -->
What sort of character is able to form an alliance without a marriage? Surely it must be someone who is focused on matters of Diplomacy. Stewardship characters are focused on internal realm management, Learning focused characters are focused on esoteric topics like medicine and religion, Intrigue characters are focused on forcing others to do what they want (a small argument could be made that this might allow a coerced alliance, but it's easier to just seduce and murder your way to victory), and while Martial characters would certainly benefit from the "free" alliance, they're too focused on military strategies and dueling etiquette. In addition, there's nothing preventing you from playing a character that dabbles in Diplomacy (only requires 2 perks, from what I can tell) before moving on to your character's education trees. It's just more efficient to play with your character's strengths than against. EDIT: Something else to consider: In CK2, you rarely need alliances. Only when you are stuck with a single county, are the target of a holy war/great holy war are alliances desperately needed. The other times can generally be solved by stockpiling a bunch of money and hiring mercs if you're *really* desperate.
