In many ways, Unity is the result of how you choose to interact with your fellow House members. We show everything regarding Unity in each Clans House view, allowing you to easily inspect your own Unity, and the Unity of other Houses. I think in practice the Clan vassals represent a couple different things, but that they themselves are not problematic. It's important to note that while these polities were ruled through a bureaucratic instrument serving the sovereign, there would still be local power brokers amongst landowners, chiefs, city lords, leaders amongst soldier communities, etc. Some might exist due to the formal recognition of the sovereign, others might rule more informally due to whatever resources and followers they have, and in the more tribal (in the social sense) socio-political structure the family/clan/tribe of these people would matter, and rulers did have to take them into consideration. Treating them all as equivalent vassals like the Feudal system that exists in-game is an abstraction, but not an unreasonable one (and to be fair, Feudalism as it exists in Europe is an abstraction). A government style built on "I appoint you so-and-so to be the tax-collector/governor of this area, but occasionally I might have to check in on and keep an eye on important people in the area that need to be buttered up/suppressed" is pretty sensible for these polities.
