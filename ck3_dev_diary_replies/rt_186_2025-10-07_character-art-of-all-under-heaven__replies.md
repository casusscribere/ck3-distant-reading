As they procured their own gear, the quality of both armour and weaponry was notably high. This system was subsequently inherited by the Sui and Tang empires. By the Song Dynasty, the fubing system was abolished, and the imperial court funded the standing army. As soldiers' equipment was provided by the government, its quality deteriorated. The Northern Song text, The Comprehensive Treatise on Military Affairs《武经总要》, states: "Armour is divided into three grades: iron armour, leather armour, and paper armour. High-ranking soldiers were equipped with iron armour, including iron helmets and chain mail, with full protection for arms and legs. The lower-ranking soldiers have iron helmets and leather armor trimmed with brocade. Horse armour also came in iron and leather varieties, some featuring silver ornaments." Legend holds that Song Dynasty paper armour was a specialised type, constructed from multiple layers of paper secured with nails, offering effective defence against arrows. However, no such artefacts have been unearthed through archaeological excavations.
