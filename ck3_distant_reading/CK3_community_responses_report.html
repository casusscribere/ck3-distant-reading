Step 4 · Themes

Six clusters of community concern, grounded in representative replies.

Register of the corpus

Where the developer diary defines systems in the second person, the responses evaluate, question and historicise them. The dominant moves are the feature request (“will there be…”, “please don't…”), the historical correction (pikes vs cavalry, what a medieval levy really was), and the comparison to CK2 / EU4 / Imperator. This is an expert, invested, sometimes pedantic audience.

Armies & Troops

army · armies · levies · troops · men-at-arms · retinue

The corpus' centre of gravity. Players interrogate the new Levy / Men-at-Arms split — what professional troops cost, whether levies are mere "meat shields", and how composition works.

“So armies don't have 3 commanders 1 for each flank anymore?”

Siege & Fortification

siege · castle · walls · besieger · trebuchet · engines

The single most-discussed mechanic. Replies dwell on siege weapons, fortified vs unfortified holdings, and the besieger-as-attacker rule.

“Will there be any restrictions on your character's actions if his main castle is under siege”

Knights & Commanders

knight · commander · martial · prestige · title

Knighthood and command draw intense debate — how powerful a knight should be relative to levies, and whether "Knight" is a title you bestow.

“I don't want to micro manage 30-40 minor titles and then these knights only have the power of 300-400 levies”

Tactics & Battle

battle · flank · tactics · advantage · attrition · supply

The loss of CK2's flanks/tactics phase is mourned repeatedly; supply and attrition as soft supply-lines are widely praised.

“Like in how army composition affected the tactics a flank used in CK2.”

Unit types & realism

cavalry · infantry · archer · pikemen · medieval · peasants

A strong historical-realism streak: long threads on whether pikes really countered cavalry, and whether medieval levies were conscripted peasants or trained freemen.

“Tactically speaking, the pike square hadn't been invented yet, and cavalry often used lances longer than the pikes of the infantry”

Comparison & meta

system · mechanic · ck2 · eu4 · imperator · warscore

Players constantly benchmark against CK2, EU4 and Imperator — the reflex of a sequel audience evaluating change against what they already know.

“I do kind of like the tactics system in CK2”

Step 8 · Critique — developer voice vs community voice

What the reading shows. Run beside the developer-diary corpus, the contrast is sharp and quantified (log-likelihood keyness): the developer over-uses precise second-person mechanics — you, your, advantage, troops, pursuit, progress, limit, damage — the register of someone defining a system. The community over-uses evaluation and speculation — like, knight, nice, would, think, yes, commanders — the register of people reacting to one. Developers specify; players appraise, historicise and compare.

What to be suspicious of. This is a single thread — the responses to one diary (War), so its vocabulary is war-shaped. The community's apparent priorities reflect what this diary surfaced, not the game as a whole. And forum repliers are the most invested slice of the audience; silent players leave no text.

What it cannot see. Tone — sarcasm, excitement, exasperation — is largely invisible to word counts. The frequent comparisons to CK2/EU4 carry an affect (nostalgia, anxiety about change) the frequencies only hint at. And every reaction emoji and up-vote was deliberately excluded to isolate the written voice.

Curation contingency

These findings describe the reply thread to CK3 Dev Diary #3 — 436 posts — not the CK3 community at large. A different diary (a faith or culture diary) would surface an entirely different community vocabulary. Scrape the remaining threads and this becomes a corpus-wide portrait of the audience; for now it is one loud, knowledgeable room.
