## Reply 49 — participant_018 · 2025-04-22 · post-30302671

To clarify Saami didn't use reindeer for mass herding at this time, that would come at a far later period. Reindeer were used in progressively more involved ways among the Saami, starting out as decoy deer and evolving into small scale stationary reindeer herding, eventually eclipsing all other forms of livelihood among the Saami in terms of cultural and societal significance. As for Karelians, I have no clue why Paradox keeps associating them with Saami people, first with the Arctic attire pack and now with the nomad game rule. Karelians were a sedentary people who didn't much differ in their lifestyle from Slavs, Balts, Finns or Germanics around them. They were involved with reindeer herding much later and got the tradition mainly from assimilated Saami people and cultural exchange but they definitely were not engaged with them during the medieval period.
