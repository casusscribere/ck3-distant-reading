Yep, we consider the Kingdom tier the 'default' in China, but the system works on modifiers - it just so happens that we split salary income base rates by vassal tier. Currently we have set these tier-modifiers to be fixed for salaries, so not adjustable - Count: 30%, Duchy: 75%, Kingdom: 100%, Empire (Minister) 150%, Empire (Landed) 200% We're still playing around with those numbers. And might add laws around these distributions. Converting Treasury is considered 'corruption', unless it's your salary! We do have the possibility in this system to not send all tax income to the top liege, which would be an effective way of keeping some gold to vassals. Additionally, the top liege doesn't have to convert all of their tax income to Treasury - in the Celestial government type we currently do though. (they're organized like that) The Budget Capacity is all the Treasury you receive that isn't already spent on fixed expenditures like Vassal Development (the standard treasury vassals receive) and Court Amenities. It's essentially your "free treasury" income. The allocation percentages determine how that "free treasury" is calculated into a base-rate for that category *at the moment of budget enactment*. The Base-Rate is the only value which is "fixed" for the duration of the budget, not the percentage. Any new recipients (Vassals of some kind) would get treasury calculated by that fixed Base Rate.

> Augustus Americanus said: Totally awesome! This is my favorite DD for this chapter thus far. The only question I have is on budgeting; you mentioned that you can manipulate your vassal contract to squeeze out more budget/salary. Individual vassal contracts can add additional multipliers on the base rate. But they will be taken into account when calculating a category's Base Rate when you enact a budget. Giving a Vassal +50% salary via an vassal contract means you pay that 50% outside of your current budget (from your unallocated budget / saved treasury effectively). But when you enact a new budget after that, the entire current state of the salary budget category is evaluated and distributed. If you then would have the same budget capacity and allocation for the Salary category, the base rate goes down a bit (more slices of the pie asked). It means all vassals earn a bit less - but the Vassal in question will still earn 50% more than the base-rate. How happy/not-happy vassals are with the budget is wholly dependent on the *change of the base-rate* of categories they are a recipient of. (on the individual level - a nice vassal contract option might offset that unhappiness with its own opinion modifier)
