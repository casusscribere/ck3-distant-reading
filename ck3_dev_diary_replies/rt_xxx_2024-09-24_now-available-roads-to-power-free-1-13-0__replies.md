You are misunderstanding this. The selection pool is not ALL commanders in the realm but the specific ones leading armies at the battle. This will prevent armies from losing battles just because the first commander to initiate battle was an untalented idiot. So for example, lets say a Byzantine army (army 1) engages a Crusader army on hilly terrain and starts the battle with a 20 advantage commander with the Open Terrain Expert trait (gives +4 advantage to plains, farmlands, and steppe Terrain). The total advantage of the commander is still 20 because the Open Terrain Expert trait does not activate here due to the hills Terrain. Now lets say a second nearby Byzantine army (army 2) comes to reinforce army 1 in the battle. The commander is 18 advantage but he does have the Rough Terrain Expert trait (+4 advantage to hills, mountains, and wetlands Terrain) which boosts his total advantage in this battle to 22. During the next tick of the battle, the army 1 commander will step down and the army 2 commander will take over in command of the total forces (army 1 and army 2) for this specific battle because the army 2 commander has more advantage. This applies to all allied armies so the army 2 commander can be that of an allied army as long as that army is present at the battle. There is zero teleporting happening here.
